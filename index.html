My journey as a coder

Feb 2022

In early February 2022, just before my class 9 final exams, I fell ill with a prolonged fever (later identified as a viral infection) and was bedridden for almost a month. This left me with persistent foot pain for nearly 20 more days, which made it hard to walk or do much physically. During that time, all I could do was study. While going through my computer book, I read a chapter on IoT and the web-things like how websites work, the concept of the WWW, and internet communication. That section triggered something in me-I got deeply curious about websites. So, I started researching more, completely self-taught using the internet. That curiosity turned into passion as I began learning HTML on my own, with no tutor-just me, my phone, and a deep desire to explore this world.

March - May 2022

After HTML, I quickly moved to CSS and styled my first full website. Though I didn't deploy it, it gave me confidence and a real sense of progress.

July - Dec 2022

While studying for class 10, I kept improving my skills - building multiple practice websites and exploring basic JavaScript concepts on the side.

Jan - Mar 2023

I built and deployed my first personal portfolio website as a frontend coder: user173432.github.io/me. This was a huge milestone for me!

Mar 18, 2023

Right after my class 10 board exams, I focused fully on JavaScript - diving into variables, functions, DOM, and basic operations.

Now (2025)

I'm in class 11, preparing for the 2027 JEE exam, and learning JavaScript more deeply - with a goal to grow from a frontend coder into a full-stack developer.
